Target Speech Extraction in matlab

In this project, we extract a target speech signal from a noisy three-channel acoustic mixture. The mixture consists of three far-field directional sources: a target English-speaking female and two interfering speakers (a German-speaking female and an English-speaking male). Additionally, uncorrelated white Gaussian noise is present in all three microphone channels. The processing pipeline involves three main stages: direction of arrival (DOA) estimation, spatial filtering via beamforming, and post-filtering for speech enhancement.

UART IMPLEMENTATION
ON FPGA

In this project, we implement UART on an FPGA. The UART consists of a transmitter and receiver, each using two counters. It is designed and simulated in Verilog using Vivado and then implemented on an Artix-7 FPGA on a BASYS 3 board. The functionality is tested via the loopback method, using Tera Term as a terminal emulator to send data from a PC to the FPGA and receive it back through the USB-UART bridge.
